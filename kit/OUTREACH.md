## The daily routine (20–30 minutes)

1. Open Google Maps. Search "<trade> <town> MA" — e.g. "landscaper Weymouth MA".
2. Look for businesses with **no website** (Maps shows a website button when they
   have one — you want the ones where it's missing, or where it links to Facebook).
3. Add 5 of them to `tracker.csv`: name, trade, town, where to contact them
   (Facebook page, phone for texting, or email if listed).
4. Send each one Message A. That's it. Done for the day.

When someone replies, paste their reply to Claude and you'll get the response to
send. When 3–5 prospects in one trade are queued, tell Claude and a personalized
demo gets built for each before you message them (demo-first works even better —
then you lead with Message B).

## Replies you'll get, and what to do

- **"How much?"** → "$300 one time. That covers everything — design, your own
  domain setup, a quote form that texts you, and launch. No subscriptions."
- **"We actually have a website."** -> "Ah — it isn't linked on your Google
  Business Profile, which means people finding you on Maps never see it. Worth
  fixing either way: log into business.google.com and add it under Edit profile,
  takes two minutes. And if you ever want the site itself refreshed, you know
  where I am." (Being wrong gracefully = being helpful. Some of these turn into
  clients later.)
- **"I already get enough work."** → "Totally fair — most of my clients did too.
  The site mostly just stops the leads you're already getting from calling the
  next guy on Google. Offer stands if that ever changes."
- **"Can you add [thing]?"** → "Almost certainly — send me what you have in
  mind." Then paste it to Claude.
- **Silence (most of them).** → One follow-up after 4–5 days: "No pressure —
  want me to build the free draft, or should I take [BUSINESS NAME] off my
  list?" Then stop. Never send a third message.

## Rules that keep this clean and legal

- Facebook DMs and business emails listed publicly: fine. **Do not text-blast
  personal cell numbers from purchased lists** — one-off texts to a number the
  business itself publishes as its contact line are normal business outreach,
  but keep it individual, honest, and stop immediately if asked.
- Never claim clients you don't have. "Sample site" means sample site.
- When money is close: Claude sets up a Stripe payment link (you'll need a free
  Stripe account — 10 minutes) and delivers the site. Keep every receipt;
  this income is taxable.

## The math to keep you sane

Expect ~1 sale per 30–50 messages. That is NORMAL and GOOD. At 25 messages/week,
$1,000 (3–4 clients) takes roughly 4–8 weeks. The system fails only one way:
the messages stop going out.
